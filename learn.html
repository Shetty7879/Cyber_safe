Password Safety & Account Protection

Securing your online accounts acts as the first line of defense against cybercriminals. The foundation of good digital hygiene begins with strong, unique passwords for every service.

Pro Tip: Use a combination of at least 12 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Avoid common words or personal information like birthdates.

Best Practices: Do’s and Don’ts

DO:

  • Use passwords with at least 8–12 characters
  • Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Use a unique password for each account
  • Enable two-factor authentication where available
  • Change passwords if a breach is suspected

DON’T:

  • Reuse the same password across multiple sites
  • Share passwords or OTPs with anyone
  • Use personal details (name, DOB, phone number)
  • Save passwords in plain text
  • Click suspicious links asking to reset passwords

Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a deceptive practice where cybercriminals send fraudulent messages (via email, SMS, or social media) designed to trick you into revealing sensitive information or installing malware.

Red Flag: The message creates a false sense of urgency (e.g., "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!").

Best Practices: Do’s and Don’ts

DO:

  • Verify the sender’s email address carefully
  • Check website URLs before clicking links
  • Use official apps or websites for logins
  • Enable email spam filters
  • Report phishing attempts to official platforms

DON’T:

  • Click on suspicious links or attachments
  • Share OTPs, passwords, or bank details
  • Trust urgent messages asking for immediate action
  • Download files from unknown sources
  • Assume messages are genuine because they look official

Cyberbullying Prevention

Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can happen on social media, messaging apps, gaming platforms, and mobile phones.

Action Plan: STOP, BLOCK, and TELL. Do not retaliate. Block the offender and report the behavior to the platform or a trusted adult.

Best Practices: Do’s and Don’ts

DO:

  • Block and report cyberbullying behavior
  • Save evidence such as messages or screenshots
  • Speak to a trusted adult, teacher, or parent
  • Use platform reporting tools responsibly
  • Support others who are being bullied online

DON’T:

  • Respond angrily or engage with bullies
  • Share hurtful messages or images
  • Participate in online harassment
  • Blame yourself for cyberbullying
  • Ignore repeated abusive behavior

Privacy Protection

Your personal data is valuable. Over-sharing online can lead to identity theft, stalking, and digital impersonation.

Golden Rule: Once something is posted online, it is almost impossible to completely remove. Think before you post.

Best Practices: Do’s and Don’ts

DO:

  • Review privacy settings on social media regularly
  • Share personal information only on trusted platforms
  • Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
  • Keep software and apps updated
  • Log out from shared or public devices

DON’T:

  • Share personal details like address, phone number, or ID online
  • Accept friend requests from unknown people
  • Allow unnecessary app permissions
  • Post sensitive photos or documents publicly
  • Use public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions without protection
