Cyber Safety for Parents

Help parents understand online risks faced by children and learn how to guide, protect, and support them in safe and responsible internet use.

Common Risks for Children Online

  • Exposure to inappropriate or harmful content
  • Cyberbullying
  • Inappropriate content
  • Gaming and chat-based scams
  • Social media pressure

Best Practices: Do’s and Don’ts for Parents

DO:

  • Talk openly with children about online activities
  • Set age-appropriate privacy and parental controls
  • Encourage children to report uncomfortable online behavior
  • Monitor screen time and app usage responsibly
  • Teach children never to share personal information or OTPs

DON’T:

  • Ignore changes in a child’s online behavior
  • Share children’s personal photos publicly without consent
  • Allow unrestricted access to unknown apps or websites
  • Scare children about the internet instead of educating them
  • Dismiss online bullying or threats as harmless

Age-Wise Online Safety Guidance

  • Establish basic internet safety rules
  • Ensure adult supervision during screen time
  • Use parental controls to restrict access to kids-only apps
  • Discuss responsible social media usage and privacy settings
  • Educate them about cyberbullying and how to report it
  • Warn them against gaming scams and interacting with unknown players
  • Discuss online relationships and the dangers of sharing intimate photos
  • Educate them to spot financial frauds and phishing links
  • Warn them about fake job scams and identity theft tricks

Talk to Your Child – Conversation Starters

Use these supportive, open-ended questions to check in on their digital lives without causing them to become defensive:

  • "What’s your favorite app or game right now? Can you show me how it works?"
  • "Have you or your friends ever seen anything online that made you feel upset or weird?"
  • "How do you decide what rules to follow when talking to people online?"
  • "If someone was being mean to you online, what would you do?"

Warning Signs to Watch For

Sudden behavioral changes can indicate that a child is experiencing cyberbullying, grooming, or exposure to inappropriate content. Watch for:

⚠️ Suddenly turning off the screen or hiding the device when a parent walks in.
⚠️ Reluctance to go to school or participate in social activities.
⚠️ Withdrawing from family, friends, or usual hobbies.
⚠️ Unexplained mood swings, anger, or sadness after using devices.
⚠️ Receiving unexpected gifts, packages, or calls from unknown numbers.

What Parents Should Do If Something Goes Wrong

1

Stay Calm and Listen

Do not get angry or blame the child. Reacting negatively might make them hide future issues. Listen to their whole story.

2

Preserve Evidence

Take screenshots, save messages, emails, and URLs. Do not delete the abusive content immediately as it serves as proof.

3

Block and Report

Block the offending user on the platform. Use built-in reporting tools on the social media, game, or app.

4

Seek Official Support

If the child is threatened or scammed, report it to the National Cyber Crime portal or call the official helpline.
