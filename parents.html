Cyber Safety for Parents

Help parents understand online risks faced by children and learn how to guide, protect, and support them in safe and responsible internet use.

Common Risks for Children Online

  • Exposure to inappropriate or harmful content
  • Cyberbullying
  • Inappropriate content
  • Gaming and chat-based scams
  • Social media pressure

Best Practices: Do’s and Don’ts for Parents

DO:

  • Talk openly with children about online activities
  • Set age-appropriate privacy and parental controls
  • Encourage children to report uncomfortable online behavior
  • Monitor screen time and app usage responsibly
  • Teach children never to share personal information or OTPs

DON’T:

  • Ignore changes in a child’s online behavior
  • Share children’s personal photos publicly without consent
  • Allow unrestricted access to unknown apps or websites
  • Scare children about the internet instead of educating them
  • Dismiss online bullying or threats as harmless

Talking to Your Child About Online Safety

  • Encourage open and honest conversations
  • Listen without judgment or anger
  • Ask about apps, games, and friends they interact with online
  • Explain online risks in simple, age-appropriate language
  • Reassure children they can report problems without fear
  • Discuss what to do if something feels uncomfortable online

Age-Wise Online Safety Guidance

Ages 8–12

  • Use parental controls and supervised browsing
  • Teach children not to share personal information
  • Limit screen time and app downloads
  • Explain safe behavior in games and chat features

Ages 13–16

  • Discuss social media privacy settings
  • Talk about cyberbullying and peer pressure
  • Encourage strong passwords and account security
  • Explain risks of sharing photos or personal details

Ages 17+

  • Discuss digital footprint and online reputation
  • Educate about online scams and phishing
  • Encourage responsible social media use
  • Teach safe online transactions and data protection
Help & Reporting: If your child faces online abuse, cyberbullying, or scams, collect evidence and report the issue through official channels.
